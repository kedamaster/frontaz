STD and STI Prevention and Treatment

Prevention

  • Use condoms during all sexual activities.
  • Get tested regularly, at least once a year or before each new partner.
  • Limit the number of sexual partners.
  • Consider vaccination for certain STIs, such as HPV and Hepatitis B.
  • Be educated about STIs and their symptoms.
  • Practice mutual monogamy with an uninfected partner.
  • Avoid sharing personal items that may come into contact with bodily fluids.
  • Discuss sexual health openly with partners.
  • Consider using dental dams for oral sex.
  • Abstain from sexual activity if you suspect you may have an STI.

Treatment

  • Consult a healthcare provider for testing and treatment.
  • Use antibiotics as prescribed for bacterial infections like chlamydia, gonorrhea, and syphilis.
  • Manage viral infections with antiviral medications (e.g., herpes, HIV).
  • Complete all medications prescribed, even if symptoms improve.
  • Inform all recent sexual partners if you test positive.
  • Abstain from sexual activity during treatment.
  • Return for follow-up testing to ensure the infection has cleared.
  • Consider counseling or support groups to deal with emotional aspects.
  • Adopt lifestyle changes to boost overall health and immune system.
  • Learn about potential complications and long-term management for chronic STIs.
